ProteinBatcher Visualizer

Standardized, design-aware downstream analysis of label-free quantitative proteomics (DIA-NN outputs). Upload your data, define contrasts, and run the full pipeline in a single click.

What does the pipeline do?

1. Filtering — Before any statistics, proteins that are too sparse are removed. A protein is kept only if it is detected in at least X% of samples within at least one experimental condition (the threshold is set in Parameters). This prevents unreliable proteins from inflating false-discovery rates.

2. Imputation — Missing intensity values are filled in using a two-step strategy: - Mean imputation: if a protein is partially missing within a condition (below the imputation threshold), missing values are replaced by the mean of the observed values for that protein in that condition. This is appropriate for random, low-level missingness. - LDV (Left-censored Distribution Value): if a protein is entirely absent in a condition, it is likely below the detection limit. Missing values are drawn randomly from the low-intensity tail of the global distribution. Proteins imputed as LDV in both groups of a contrast are automatically excluded from the BH-FDR correction to avoid spurious significance.

3. Differential testing — A linear model is fitted with limma using the formula you specify. The design can include batch effects, donor effects, and interaction terms. The result for each protein is a log₂ fold change, a raw p-value, and a BH-adjusted p-value (FDR).

4. Interpreting the three output types
  • All proteins volcano — Shows the estimated condition effect for every tested protein, regardless of whether that effect is stable across batches or donors. This is your first overview of the data.
  • Common (stable) effect — Proteins whose condition response is consistent across the secondary factor (e.g. batch, sex). These are the most biologically robust hits: the effect does not depend on which batch or subgroup you look at.
  • Interaction effect — Proteins whose condition response differs significantly between factor levels (e.g. the IL13 effect is strong in batch day1 but absent in day2). These are not false positives — they reflect genuine heterogeneity and may be biologically interesting, but should be interpreted carefully.

Interpretation & Support
Interpreting results
  • All proteins volcano — global condition effect; includes batch-dependent proteins.
  • Common effect — proteins with a consistent condition response. Most biologically robust.
  • Interaction effect — proteins whose response differs significantly between factor levels.
  • Deregulogram — emphasises direction-changing or magnitude-shifting proteins. More restrictive than the interaction volcano.

log₂FC Distribution by Imputation Category — Density

Use the Category Filter in the sidebar to focus on specific imputation patterns. Hover each curve for per-category counts and DE numbers.

Interpretation: Categories with LDV in one group and nothing in the other (ldv-none, none-ldv) typically show shifted distributions — this is expected and biologically meaningful (proteins absent in one condition). ldv-ldv proteins should show no significant results (excluded from BH correction by design). A shift in mean-mean or none-none would indicate a genuine condition effect.

log₂FC Distribution by Imputation Category — Boxplot

Box spans IQR, whiskers extend to 1.5×IQR; outliers shown as individual points. Hover an outlier to identify the protein. Large median shifts in none-none confirm genuine biology.
